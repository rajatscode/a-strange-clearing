The point of this site is not to make me easy to understand. I don’t really
experience “Rajat” as a bio, a career arc, a contact form, and some projects.
That is just the name people use so they can think about me without having to
model an entire inner life. I do the same thing to them. What I want is a place
where parts of that inner life can surface without getting converted into
personal-brand slop: technical ideas, tools, essays, jokes, moral intuitions,
fragments, whatever. The posts should be shareable because ideas should move.
The space around them should still ask something of you. No puzzle. No IQ test.
Just enough friction that people who only skim and extract bounce off, while
people with patience, curiosity, and some aliveness can find their way in. The
basic bet is that attention has ethics. Approach things carefully and the world
opens. Treat everything as content and it closes.

The clearing is a side-view tree-world in a dark void. You are a centipede
moving across leaves and branches. Body length is reach, wealth, and
vulnerability. Leaves are the infrastructure of the world. Star-rain keeps them
alive, lit, and traversable. When the sky goes dark, they wither and fall away.
The tree gets smaller. Other centipedes live there too. Get close to one and a
thread forms. A pot starts. Both of you stake pieces of your bodies. The thread
cycles red and blue. Blue means cooperation. Red means defection. Cooperation is
modest. If both agents cooperate, each grows a little. Golden dust rises. Stars
form. Rain returns. Leaves recover. The world gets slightly more habitable.
Defection pays faster. The defector steals body, and black dust rises with the
theft. Stars get eaten. Rain thins. Leaves die. The tree becomes darker,
narrower, meaner. Each choice changes the social weather. Defecting makes other
centipedes a little more likely to defect later. Cooperating makes them a little
more willing to cooperate. The point is not score-maxing. You can always cheat
the world, and cheating often works. Every betrayal helps create a world where
betrayal is normal. You have to act like you live in the world you wish existed,
because that is how it starts existing.

The links are things the world becomes willing to show you. Near the bottom,
when you are small or new or the world is dark, you can find surface-level
things: my name, a few obvious traces, maybe one or two low-stakes leaves.
Enough to know the place is real. The more substantial artifacts live higher up,
deeper in, or in parts of the tree that only become reachable when the ecology
can support you. You get access by making the world brighter, more cooperative,
more traversable, more legible. Golden leaves, little Zelda-fairy things, seeds,
shrines, whatever the final shape is. They are permissions. When you find one,
the game freezes, the world blurs behind it, and the artifact opens as a normal
page: text, images, HTML, code, diagrams, whatever. You can read it normally. It
should still feel like it came from somewhere. Keep the world beautiful and the
site gets more generous. Defect, thrash, extract, poison it, and the site does
not scold you. It becomes the world you deserve: dimmer, less trusting, less
navigable, less willing to reveal what it protects.

The artifacts should remember being found. When a golden leaf or firefly
appears, it is minting a specific discovery: this artifact, found in this
world-state, through this path, by this visitor. Opening it gives you a share
link. That link should carry the discovery, not just the slug. If someone
follows it, they can read the artifact directly, because ideas should travel.
The link should still know it came through someone else’s passage. The ecology
can acknowledge that quietly: a ghost centipede in the canopy, a star-trail, a
distant dragon shape moving above the tree, something like that. Provenance made
visible. Discovery should be durable. A link should not just say “this exists.”
It should say “this was found.”
