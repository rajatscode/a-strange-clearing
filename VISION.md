This site is not meant to make me legible. I don’t experience “Rajat” as a bio,
a career, a contact form, and some work. That’s just scaffolding—a name people
use to avoid modeling an entire inner life. I do the same to them. What I want is
a place where the interior can surface without being converted into personal
brand: ideas, tools, essays, jokes, moral intuitions, fragments, whatever. These
should travel because ideas move. The space around them should ask something in
return. Not a puzzle. Not an IQ test. Enough resistance that shallow extraction
bounces off, while patience, curiosity, and aliveness find their way through.
The deep bet is that attention is ethical. Approach carefully and the world
opens. Treat everything as content and it closes.

The clearing is a side-view tree-world in a dark void. You are a centipede
moving across leaves and branches. Your body is reach, wealth, and vulnerability.
Leaves are the infrastructure. Star-rain keeps them alive, lit, traversable. When
the sky darkens, they wither and fall. The tree shrinks. Other centipedes live
here. Get close to one and a thread forms. A pot opens. Both of you stake pieces
of your body. The thread cycles red and blue. Blue is cooperation. Red is
defection. Cooperation is modest. Both cooperate: each grows a little. Golden
dust rises, stars form, rain returns, leaves recover. The world becomes slightly
more habitable. Defection pays faster. The defector takes body; black dust rises.
Stars fade. Rain thins. Leaves die. The tree becomes darker, narrower, meaner.
Each choice changes the ecology. Defecting makes other centipedes more likely to
defect next. Cooperating makes them more willing to cooperate. This is not about
score. You can always cheat the world—cheating often works. But every betrayal
normalizes betrayal. Every defection makes defection more reasonable. You have to
act as if the world you wish to live in already exists, because that is how it
begins to exist.

The links are things the world becomes willing to show you. Near the roots,
when you are small or the world is dark, you find surface things: my name, a few
traces, maybe one low-stakes leaf. Enough to know the place exists. Deeper
artifacts live higher, further in, in parts of the tree only reachable when the
ecology can hold you. You earn access by making the world brighter, more
cooperative, more traversable, more legible. Golden leaves, seeds, shrines,
whatever shapes emerge. These are permissions—not locked doors, but invitations
the world learns to extend. When you find one, the game pauses, the world blurs,
and the artifact opens as a normal page: text, images, code, whatever. You read
it ordinarily. It still carries where it came from. Keep the world generous
and the site is generous. Poison it—defect, extract, thrash—and the site asks
nothing of you. It becomes what you deserve: dimmer, less trusting, less
navigable, less willing to show what it knows.

Artifacts remember how they were found. When a golden leaf appears, it mints a
specific discovery: this artifact, in this world-state, through this path, by
this visitor. Opening it gives a share link. That link carries the discovery, not
just the slug. If someone follows it, they read the artifact directly, because
ideas move. The link still knows it came through someone’s passage. The
ecology acknowledges it quietly: a ghost centipede in the canopy, a star-trail,
something like that. Provenance made visible. Discovery should be durable. A link
should not say “this exists.” It should say “this was found.”
